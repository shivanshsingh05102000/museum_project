About Us

The British Museum is exceptional in that it brings together civilizations from across continents and oceans under one roof. No other museum is in charge of collections of the same range, depth, beauty, and importance. With its eight million items, we may examine the astounding variety of human cultures—from little villages to great empires—discover the numerous interpretations and forms that people have given to every facet of existence, and see how intricately linked they are. The British Museum was established in 1753, and its doors were first opened in 1759. It was the first national museum that was available to tourists from all around the world and included every branch of human knowledge. Since its inception, the Museum has been guided by the ideas and principles of the Enlightenment: open discussion, scientific inquiry, critical examination of all presumptions, advancement, and tolerance. The Museum is motivated by an unquenchable thirst for information, a strong confidence in artefacts as trustworthy witnesses and records of human history, solid research, and the desire to share and develop that knowledge.
